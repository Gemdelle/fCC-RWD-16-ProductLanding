Species

Chilasa clytia clytia

$08.00
  • Common Name: Common Mime
  • Genus: Chilasa
  • Pattern: Stripes
  • Wingspan: 80 - 90 mm.
  • Flower: Cinnamomum iners

The butterfly mimics the Danainae species for protection against predators.

After 11-12 days, the pupa turns black as the development within the pupal case draws to a close.

The 5th instar lasts for 4 days, and the body length reaches up to 55mm. Toward the end of this instar, the body gradually shortens in length.

The egg takes 3 days to hatch. The young caterpillar eats its way out of the mature egg, and then proceeds to finish up the rest of the egg shell.

Leptosia nina malayana

$13.00
  • Common Name: Psyche
  • Genus: Leptosia
  • Pattern: Stripes
  • Wingspan: 36 - 46 mm.
  • Flower: Cleome rutidosperma

The adults are small and have rounded white wings. Above, the wings are entirely white except for a black apical border and a black subapical spot on the forewing.

After about 4.5-5 days of development, the pupal skin turns translucent as the development within the pupal case comes to an end.

The 5th instar lasts for 2.5-3.0 days, and the body length reaches up to 20-21mm. On the last day of the 5th instar, the body of the caterpillar shortens and changes to a bright green shade.

The egg takes about 2.5-3 days to hatch. The newly hatched eats the empty egg shell for its first meal, and then moves on to eat the leaf lamina.

Cethosia cyane

$10.00
  • Common Name: Leopard Lacewing
  • Genus: Cethosia
  • Pattern: Spots
  • Wingspan: 75 - 80 mm.
  • Flower: Passiflora foetida

Above, the male is orange with apical two-thirds of the forewing black with a white and oblique band lying within. The female is mostly similar but with the orange background replaced with a pale yellowish to whitish coloration.

After about 6 days of development, the mature pupa are darkened, especially in the wing spad.

The 5th instar lasts 4-5 days and the body length reaching up to 39mm

Each egg takes about 5-6 days to hatch. The infant caterpillar nibbles away a small portion of the egg shell and pushes its way through the crack.
